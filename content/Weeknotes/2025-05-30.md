I read "[On Pair Programming][]" by Birgitta BÃ¶ckeler and Nina Siessegger.
The article describes how to pair,
the reasons why you should and shouldn't pair,
and concludes that you should pair judiciously.
It isn't a quick read
-- it's over 9,000 words long --
but it's well-structured,
with clear headings and sub-headings.

I wasn't surprised by the article,
but I'm keen on pairing.
It demonstrates to people who are less-than keen on pairing that well-respected people who work for well-respected organisations
have thought about the benefits and challenges of pairing,
and conclude that pairing (mostly) works.

Lucy recommended the article in Slack;
not to me specifically, but to the tech group in general.
Pairing isn't the "sensible default" in the tech group.
I'm coming to the conclusion that it should be.
